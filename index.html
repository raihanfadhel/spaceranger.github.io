Hello!

My name is Muhammad Raihannafi Fadhel, or you can just call me Raihan (pronounced: Rah-ee-Hahn) or Fadhel (pronounced: Fah-dell). I know it's a bit confusing, but for Fadhel, it's "Fah", like in "father", and "dell", like the computer brand.

Since high school, I have been really intrigued by Physics and actively joined science competitions, particularly as one of the delegates of my high school in the Astronomy Olympiad (OSN) for two years straight. This passion has persisted until I enrolled in a physics undergraduate program at Universitas Gadjah Mada (UGM). The pinnacle of my career was in 2024, where I was awarded a Bronze Medal in Physics at Olimpiade Nasional Matematika dan Ilmu Pengetahuan Alam - Perguruan Tinggi (ONMIPA-PT). Actually, I wasn't quite satisfied with the result, and deep down in my heart, I felt I should've earned more, but still... Alhamdulillah. However, because my final year was already in front of my very eyes, and kind of haunted me, I decided to end my olympiad career and focus on my thesis project.

My undergraduate thesis project has opened me up to the world of particle physics, and under Dr. Chalis Setyadi's supervision, I became deeply immersed in the research of phenomenological high-energy physics. To put it simply, I examined the behaviour of the internal structure of the proton in high-energy scattering, particularly in exclusive vector meson production. This small but meaningful project has allowed me to engage further in academia, and led me to present my research at a Southeast Asian Workshop on Nuclear and Hadron Physics and to publish my first-ever manuscript based on my results in Modern Physics Letters A.

Recently, I was admitted to join the Joint European Master Degree in Nuclear Physics, starting in October 2026, with an Erasmus Mundus Scholarship. Hopefully, under this program, I will be able to deepen my understanding of the theoretical and computational aspects of nuclear and particle physics, as well as sharpen my research skills to contribute further to the field, to stay at the forefront of experimental and future collider programs (Bismillah!).
